\section{Executive Summary}

\textbf{Recommendation:} DockMaster should develop and launch \highlight{EstimateIQ}, an AI-powered service estimate generator that transforms the labor-intensive process of creating marine repair estimates into a 5-minute workflow.

\subsection*{The Opportunity}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Marine repair shops lose an estimated \textbf{\$14,667/month per 5-technician shop} in billable hours due to administrative inefficiency
    \item Service writers spend \textbf{15--30 minutes per estimate} manually researching parts, looking up labor guides, and cross-referencing vessel specifications
    \item The marina management software market has effectively zero AI penetration
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{The Solution}
EstimateIQ leverages DockMaster's historical work order data across 1,000+ marinas to generate accurate, data-driven service estimates in minutes. Using a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) architecture with real-time parts pricing integration, EstimateIQ recommends labor tasks, parts, and quantities based on patterns learned from thousands of similar completed jobs.

\section{Problem Statement}

\subsection{The Core Challenge}

Marine service departments operate in a high-complexity environment where creating accurate estimates is both critical and time-consuming. Unlike automotive repair with standardized flat-rate guides, marine repair involves combinatorial complexity across multiple variables:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Vessel type and LOA (Length Overall)
    \item Engine make, model, and age
    \item Regional factors (saltwater vs. freshwater, climate)
    \item Seasonal timing and demand patterns
    \item Service type and scope
\end{itemize}

This complexity creates significant operational bottlenecks that directly impact revenue and customer satisfaction.

\subsection{Quantified Pain Points}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}L{5cm}L{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Pain Point} & \textbf{Impact} \\
\midrule
Manual paperwork and data entry & 2--3 hours per technician daily [1] \\
Estimate approval delays & Average 45 minutes per job [1] \\
Lost billable hours per shop & \$14,667/month (5-technician shop) [2] \\
Supply chain delays affecting maintenance & 60\% of ship owners experience delays [3] \\
Field service inefficiency impact & 68\% report unplanned downtime hits revenue [1] \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Current Workflow Inefficiencies}

The estimate creation process suffers from multiple friction points:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Quote Creation and Rework:} ``Marine shop teams create estimates repeatedly for different customers, often starting from scratch because they lack reusable templates.''
    
    \item \textbf{Waiting on Approvals:} ``Technicians often complete a repair step and then pause while they wait for customer approval, often buried in texts, voicemails, or emails.''
    
    \item \textbf{Inconsistent Pricing:} New and seasonal staff lack experience to create accurate estimates, leading to pricing discrepancies and margin erosion.
    
    \item \textbf{Lost Revenue from Slow Response:} Boat owners report long wait times for estimates, leading to lost jobs when competitors respond faster.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Why AI Is the Right Solution}

This problem cannot be solved by better templates alone. The complexity of marine service estimation involves:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Tens of thousands of meaningful variable combinations} across vessel types, engine configurations, and service types
    \item \textbf{Regional and seasonal patterns} that affect labor times and parts availability
    \item \textbf{Historical pricing intelligence} that no single service writer can hold in memory
\end{itemize}

DockMaster's data asset---work order history across 1,000+ marinas---contains patterns that only machine learning can effectively extract and apply. This creates a \textbf{network effect}: every work order completed across the customer base improves the model for every customer, building a competitive moat no competitor can replicate without equivalent data.

\section{Solution Overview}

\subsection{What is EstimateIQ?}

EstimateIQ is an AI-powered service estimate generator that transforms marine repair estimation from a 15--30 minute manual process into a 5-minute intelligent workflow.

\subsection{How It Works}

\subsubsection*{Input}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Vessel identifier (or manual entry of specs: LOA, engine make/model, year)
    \item Service request description (free text or category selection)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Processing}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item AI analyzes the request against historical work order patterns
    \item Retrieves similar completed jobs from DockMaster's network
    \item Matches vessel-specific, regional, and seasonal factors
    \item Cross-references real-time parts pricing from PartSmart integration
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Output}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Recommended line items (labor tasks with hour estimates)
    \item Parts recommendations with quantities and current pricing
    \item Confidence indicator for each recommendation
    \item Range presentation (low/expected/high)
    \item Comparison reference: ``Based on 47 similar jobs on 25--30' vessels with Volvo D4 engines''
    \item Flagged upsell considerations
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Key Differentiators}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering

Cross-marina learning & Yes & No & No \\

\subsection{Human-in-the-Loop Design}

EstimateIQ is designed as a \textbf{recommendation tool}, not an autonomous system:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item AI suggests estimates; human service writers review and approve
    \item All recommendations can be modified before sending to customers
    \item Service writer expertise is augmented, not replaced
    \item This approach reduces liability and builds trust during adoption
\end{itemize}

\section{Technical Architecture}

\subsection{Core Components}

\subsubsection{Data Pipeline}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item ETL process extracts historical work orders from DockMaster database
    \item Work orders are processed, cleaned, and embedded into vector representations
    \item Vector database enables semantic similarity search across millions of historical jobs
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) Engine}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item When a new estimate request arrives, the system retrieves the most similar historical jobs
    \item Retrieved context is passed to the LLM along with the current request
    \item LLM generates structured estimate recommendations grounded in real data
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{PartSmart Integration}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Real-time API connection to PartSmart catalog
    \item All parts suggestions are validated against current catalog
    \item Pricing is pulled in real-time to ensure accuracy
    \item \textbf{Critical:} System never generates/hallucinates part numbers---all suggestions grounded in catalog
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Confidence Scoring}
Each recommendation includes a confidence score based on:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Number of similar historical jobs found
    \item Variance in historical labor times
    \item Recency of similar jobs
    \item Match quality on vessel specifications
\end{itemize}
